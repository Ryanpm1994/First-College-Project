Slightly Harder

Numbers bigger than ten are useful for telling the time and many other things.

Can you see a pattern below?

onze - eleven

douze - twelve

treize - thirteen

quatorze - fourteen

quinze - fifteen

seize - sixteen

Yes, they all end -ZE.

There's another pattern coming up from seventeen - nineteen. Can you work it out?

dix-sept - seventeen

dix-huit - eighteen

dix-neuf - nineteen

That's right! You're saying dix-sept - literally 'ten seven' and so on.

So, we've got to twenty! That's vingt.

vingt - twenty

Il est cinq heures vingt. - It’s twenty-past five.

That is you now up to twenty now it time to learn a bit more but the hardest part is now over

Once you arrive at twenty, it becomes a matter of adding the number to the prefix -- as in English. In addition to un, cinq , vingt and all of the ante endings are nasal sounds.

20 is vingt and is pronounced as van (nasal)

21 is vingt-et un and is pronounced vant-ay-uh

22 is vingt-deux and is pronounced as van-duhr

30 is trente and is pronounced as tront

31 is trent-et-un and is pronounced as tront-ay-uh

40 is quarante and is pronounced as karont (nasal)

50 is cinquante and is pronounced as sank-ont (nasal)

60 is soixante and is pronounced as swa-sont (nasal)

80 is quatre-vingts and is pronounced as katr-van (nasal)

100 is cent and is pronounced as son (nasal)

1,000 is mille and is pronounced as meel

The Special Case of 70 and 90

Now septante just doesn't sound right and may even sound a bit too much like it's predecessor

soixante. Therefore, from 60-80 is just like 1-20.At 70 add ten and keep going until 80:

70 is soixante-dix and is pronounced as swa-sont-dees (nasal)

At 90 add ten to 'quatre-vingts' and continue until 100

90 is quartre-vingts-dix and is pronounced as kat-ra-van-dees (nasal)
